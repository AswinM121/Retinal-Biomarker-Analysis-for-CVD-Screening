Ophthalmic Biomarkers for Cardiovascular Disease (CVD) Risk Prediction

Ophthalmic CVD Indicators are emerging as powerful non-invasive tools for early prediction of cardiovascular risk. This section introduces a novel parameter derived from retinal image classification, enabling risk stratification using key ophthalmic features.

STARE Dataset Categories

To generate comprehensive feature maps, we use the STARE dataset, which contains retinal fundus images labeled across thirteen diagnostic categories:

  • Normal (N)
  • Coat's Disease (CD)
  • Macroaneurysm (MA)
  • Drusen (D)
  • Background Diabetic Retinopathy (BDR)
  • Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy (PDR)
  • Choroidal Neovascularization (CNV)
  • Arteriosclerotic Retinopathy (AR)
  • Hypertensive Retinopathy (HTR)
  • Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (CRVO)
  • Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion (BRVO)
  • Hemi-Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (HCRVO)
  • Retinal Artery Occlusions (RAO, BRAO)

The feature map generated from these labeled categories is integrated into the system's final classification pipeline to improve CVD risk prediction.

Retinal Biomarkers and Their Link to CVD

Retinal abnormalities serve as biomarkers that often correlate with underlying cardiovascular issues. Key insights from literature and clinical data support the inclusion of the following conditions as significant predictors:

Hypertensive and Arteriosclerotic Retinopathy

  • Hypertensive Retinopathy (HTR)
  • Arteriosclerotic Retinopathy (AR)

Indicate long-standing vascular stress and hypertension, both strong contributors to cardiovascular disease risk.

Diabetic Retinopathy

  • Background Diabetic Retinopathy (BDR)
  • Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy (PDR)

Reflect microvascular damage often associated with systemic diabetes and cardiovascular complications.

Choroidal Neovascularization

  • Choroidal Neovascularization (CNV)

Signals systemic vascular anomalies and increases the likelihood of cardiovascular disorders.

Retinal Vein and Artery Occlusions

  • Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (CRVO)
  • Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion (BRVO)
  • Hemi-Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (HCRVO)
  • Retinal Artery Occlusions (RAO, BRAO)

Indicate thrombotic events and impaired retinal blood flow, which may correlate with stroke risk, atherosclerosis, and heart disease.

Macroaneurysms(MA)

Macroaneurysms are large aneurysms that occur in the retinal vasculature and are associated with an increased risk of cardiovascular disease (CVD). Additionally, macroaneurysms may also be a sign of increased blood pressure and flow in the retinal vasculature, which can further contribute to the development of CVD.

These biomarkers form the Ophthalmic Biomarker Module (OBM) of the overall system and are crucial for accurate, image-based cardiovascular risk assessment. Other than these the remaining biomarkers such as Coat's Disease(CD), Drusen(D), Normal(N) which are actually not related to CVD and they are grouped under Neutral Biomakers(NBM).
