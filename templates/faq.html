Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our CVD risk assessment system

About the Assessment

What is retinal image-based CVD risk assessment?

Our system uses advanced AI technology to analyze retinal images and detect early signs of cardiovascular disease. The retina provides a unique window into your body's vascular health, allowing for non-invasive risk assessment.

How long does the assessment take?

The entire process typically takes about 3-5 minutes, including image upload and analysis. Results are available immediately after processing is complete.

Technical Details

What biomarkers do you analyze?

We analyze three key biomarkers:

  • Arteriovenous Ratio (AVR) - Indicates vascular health
  • Cup-to-Disc Ratio (CDR) - Evaluates optic nerve health
  • Fractal Dimension (FD) - Measures vascular network complexity
  • Ophthalmic biomarkers - Key indicators in predicting cardiovascular disease risk.

What image quality is required?

For optimal results, we recommend high-resolution retinal fundus images that are well-focused and properly centered. Our system includes image enhancement techniques to improve quality, but better input images lead to more accurate results.

Is my data secure?

Yes, we take data security seriously. All uploaded images and analysis results are encrypted and processed securely. We comply with healthcare data protection standards and do not share your information with third parties.

Using the System

How do I get started?

Getting started is easy:

  1. Click on the "Assessment" link in the navigation
  2. Upload your retinal fundus image
  3. Wait for the analysis to complete
  4. Review your comprehensive risk assessment report

What file formats are supported?

We support common image formats including:

  • JPEG (.jpg, .jpeg)
  • PNG (.png)
  • TIFF (.tiff, .tif)

Can I save or download my results?

Yes, after the assessment is complete, you can download a detailed PDF report containing all analysis results, biomarker measurements, and risk assessments. You can also save the processed images showing vessel segmentation and classification.
