Fractal Dimension (FD) Analysis for CVD Risk Assessment

What is Fractal Dimension?

Fractal Dimension is a non-integer metric that quantifies the complexity and density of retinal vascular branching, capturing self-similarity in vessel patterns.

Why it Matters

FD Value Interpretation Clinical Significance
Higher FD (> 1.3) Dense, healthy microvascular networks Indicates good vascular health
Lower FD (≤ 1.3) Rarefied, potentially diseased vasculature May indicate hypertension or cardiovascular disease risk

Methodology Highlights

Box Counting

Overlays grids to count vessel-filled cells at multiple scales

Minkowski–Bouligand

Dilates vessels and tracks area growth

Sandbox

Measures pixel count within expanding regions around vessel points

Key Insight

Minkowski–Bouligand yields the most accurate FD values with lowest MAE. A strong positive correlation (r ≈ 0.97) was found between FD and vessel density, reinforcing FD's role as a reliable biomarker for CVD.
